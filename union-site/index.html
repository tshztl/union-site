Timeline

A little history...

  • 1966

    First Grad Union

    A small group of graduate student workers in Madison, Wisconsin gathered to form a union; Wisconsin's TAA soon became the first graduate union to win recognition, inaugurating the grad labor movement in (Anglophone) North America.

  • 1998

    UC Grading Strike

    UC teaching assistants went on a successful grading strike during finals week for union recognition by the university.

  • 1999

    A New Union is Born

    Academic student employees voted overwhelmingly in favor of UAW representation and negotiated a first contract.

  • 2014

    Endorsement of BDS

    Rank-and-file members voted to pass a resolution honoring the Palestinian call for a boycott, divestment and sanctions (BDS) campaign against Israel. The resolution calls on the University of California and UAW International to divest from companies involved in Israeli apartheid and U.S. military aid to Israel.

  • Now

    Preparing for bargaining

    Our next task is to figure out what we want in our next contract, to be negotiated in 2018.

Parenting Working Group

Work on contract demands involving childcare, lactation stations, family leave, and more.

Our members are committed to securing better working conditions for parenting students. In support of Mothers of Color in Academia de UCLA, we demand accessible lactation stations, affordable childcare, and holistic reproductive support on campus.

You can find information here about UAW 2865's $900/quarter Childcare Reimbursement Program.

Anti-Racism Working Group

Fight anti-Black racism and Islamophobia on campus. Build solidarity with the BDS movement and the Movement for Black Lives.

Our members are committed to anti-racist organizing. In collaboration with The Undercommons , we have worked to bring attention to and fight racism on campus and in the Southland.

We are also committed to fighting Trump's Islamophobic "Muslim Ban" and helping connect members with emergency resources.

Queer and Trans* Working Group

Defend and improve contract language involving inclusive healthcare, all-gender bathrooms, and more.

Members are fighting gender and gender identity discrimination with respect to restroom access and inclusive healthcare at the Ashe center. We demand equal access to all-gender restrooms in all campus buildings. We are also fighting transphobic resistance to multi-stall all-gender restrooms on campus.

Email losangeles@uaw2865.org to join or for more information.

Sexual Harassment Working Group

Defend the rights of survivors through the grievance process, publicize the incompetence of the university and Title IX office, and challenge the ubiquity of rape culture in academia.

UCLA's campus Sexual Harassment and Sexual Violence Working Group grew out of and is a part of an all-UC campus network of activists. This network coordinates the sharing of information across campuses about policies, solutions, and obstacles to achieving a rape-free university. The working group defends students as workers and in doing so all survivors---students, faculty, and staff. With contract negotiations approaching in 2018, the members of the Working Group will take the lead in canvassing the general membership on the tools they need to defend themselves and to combat rape culture in the academic workplace. The Working Group will advocate within the union for these requirements to be given the highest priority.

The SHSV Working Group has worked closely with other groups on campus dedicated to opposing academic and collegiate rape culture: BASH (Bruins Against Sexual Harassment) and the Bruin Consent Coalition, as well as other progressive student organizations on campus. Sexual assault survivors and their allies practice active solidarity with the struggles of all oppressed and marginalized students (the majority of us). We believe the only way we win this is if we turn out for each other. Join us.

Email losangeles@uaw2865.org to join or for more information.

Stewards Network

A steward in every department and head stewards at each JC.

We encourage members to get more involved with the Union!

Attending a Monthly Membership Meeting or meeting an officer for coffee is a great place to start. If you want to step up your activism, become a Steward or Campus Steward. Stewards and Campus Stewards focus on campus and community issues and are vital to the day to day functioning of the union. Stewards keep rank-and-file in their Departments informed, organize actions, and participate in our various campaigns. As part of our Steward Network, we aim to keep Stewards plugged in to the decision-making process and recruit their colleagues to take a more active role. We provide funding for Stewards to meet-up with their colleagues and recruit future Stewards. For information of becoming a Steward or a Campus Steward, see our Bylaws.

Email losangeles@uaw2865.org for more information.

RA/GSR Campaign

Uniting GSRs and GSIs at the UCs.

While all UC graduate students are eligible to join UAW 2865, our Local only represents TAs, tutors, and readers in collective bargaining. Research Assistants and Graduate Student Researchers (RAs/GSRs) can become members of the union and participate in its decision making process, but are not covered by the contract.

The California Higher Education Employer-Employee Relations Act (HEERA) grants most UC employees the right to bargain collectively. However, the University still holds that RAs/GSRs are not employees of the University because we do not work primarily for pay, but to further our own academic interests. UAW 2865 is fighting for GSRs’ rights to be recognized alongside TAs, tutors, and readers.

If you are currently working as a GSR/RA, please fill out our survey here.
